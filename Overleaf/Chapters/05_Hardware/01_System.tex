\chapter{Hardware} \label{sec:Hardware}
 
The hardware design was governed by a small set of tightly coupled constraints that had to be resolved together rather than in isolation. The wireless communication protocol was selected first, as this choice directly determined the family of MCUs available. Beyond protocol support, the MCU was required to combine low power consumption, cost-effectiveness, and sufficient ADC measurement performance for the strain front end.
 
With the MCU fixed, the schematic was developed with an emphasis on simplicity and component economy, after which the PCB was laid out to fit within the dimensional envelope imposed by the bolt nut. This is where the strongest coupling arose, as the board outline completed with the antenna keepout and ground plane for the same limited area, so the form factor and the RF layout had to be balanced against one another rather than fixed independently. Finally, the battery was selected to satisfy the power budget set by the measurement cadence and low-power strategy, with its chemistry chosen to sustain an operational lifetime in excess of ten years while retaining sufficient capacity and pulse performance across the wide temperature range encountered in offshore wind turbine applications.
