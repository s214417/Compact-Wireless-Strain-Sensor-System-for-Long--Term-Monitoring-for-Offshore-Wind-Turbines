\chapter{Hardware} \label{sec:Hardware}

The hardware architecture is highly interdependent, requiring a systematic design approach. The starting point is the selection of a wireless communication protocol, as this directly constrains the choice of MCU. Beyond protocol support, the MCU must satisfy requirements for low power consumption, cost-effectiveness, and ADC measurement performance.

With the MCU determined, the schematic is developed with an emphasis on simplicity and efficiency, after which the PCB is designed to fit the dimensional constraints, imposed by the nut, while adhering to best practices for RF layout. The battery is selected to provide a long operational lifetime under the environmental conditions encountered in wind turbine applications.
