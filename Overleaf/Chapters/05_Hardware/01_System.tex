\chapter{Hardware} \label{sec:Hardware}

The hardware architecture of the project is highly interdependent, requiring a systematic design approach. The first step is the selection of a suitable wireless communication protocol, as this has an influence on the choice of microcontroller unit (MCU). The MCU must support the selected protocol while also meeting requirements for low power consumption, cost-effectiveness, and high ADC measurement performance.
Once a suitable MCU has been identified, the schematic can be developed with an emphasis on simplicity, functionality, and efficiency. Subsequently, the printed circuit board (PCB) will be designed to fit the dimensional constraints imposed by the nut while adhering to best practices for RF circuit implementation.
The PCB design will be optimized for low-power operation, accurate analog-to-digital conversion, reliable wireless communication, and robust performance under varying temperature and humidity conditions. 
The PCB will be powered by a battery, selected to provide long operational lifetime and dependable performance under the environmental conditions encountered in wind turbine applications. The integrated system will enable continuous monitoring and wireless transmission of sensor data while minimizing power consumption and maintenance requirements.
