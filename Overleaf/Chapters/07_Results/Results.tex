\chapter{Results} \label{sec:Results}
This chapter presents the experimental verification of the system against the design requirements set out in \Cref{sec:ProblemStatement}. The intended operating conditions, a real offshore turbine over a multi-year deployment, cannot be reproduced within the scope of this work, and the system is therefore not validated in the field but instead verified against its design targets under controlled laboratory conditions.
 
First the assembled Wheatstone half-bridge is evaluated as a complete strain-sensing element, covering its response to a controlled strain profile, its hysteresis under repeated loading, its stability at rest, and its behavior over extended runs and varying temperature. Afterwards the supporting hardware is verified, addressing the wireless link strength, the on-die temperature sensor proposed for compensation, the current consumption and resulting battery lifetime, and the per-unit manufacturing cost.
