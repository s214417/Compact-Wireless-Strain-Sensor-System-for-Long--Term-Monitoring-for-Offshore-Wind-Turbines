The microcontroller (Arduino Uno, item~4 in \cref{fig:setup}) is the central controller of the test bench, coordinating both the actuation and the measurement. It drives the digital stepper driver (item~3), which steps the stepper motor (item~2), then rotating the lead screw of the linear stage (item~1), and generating the linear travel that strains the device under test (DUT). 
To synchronize the measurements with this motion, the microcontroller sends a digital trigger to the I/O port of the source meter (Keithley 2450, item~5), so that each measurement starts at a known motor position and time stamp. The DC power supply (item~6) powers the motor through the stepper driver and can additionally supply the excitation voltage for the Wheatstone bridge.

The problem statement (\Cref{sec:ProblemStatement}) requires the sensor to be able to measure strains of at least \SI{800}{\micro\strain} over a square surface with a side length of \SI{30}{\milli\meter}. To produce this strain on the test bench, the required elongation follows from the definition of strain (\cref{eq:strain definition}, \Cref{sec:Strain}):

\begin{align}\label{eq:microStrain}
    \Delta L = \varepsilon L_{0} = \SI{800}{\micro\strain} \cdot \SI{30}{\milli\meter} = \SI{24}{\micro\meter}.
\end{align}

Realizing such a small elongation slowly and with high resolution requires a fine actuation step. The microstepping of the digital stepper driver is therefore set to its maximum of \num{25600} pulses per revolution, which yields a microstep size of:

\begin{align} \label{eq:microstep}
    \text{microstep size} = \frac{\text{travel per revolution}}{\text{pulses per revolution}} = \frac{\SI{5}{\milli\meter}}{\num{25600}} = \SI{195.3}{\nano\meter}.
\end{align}

Reaching the target elongation of \SI{24}{\micro\meter} therefore requires \num{123} microsteps, the smallest integer above the exact requirement of \num{122.9}. This results in an actual elongation of \SI{24.02}{\micro\meter}, or \SI{800.78}{\micro\strain}, which satisfies the requirement of at least \SI{800}{\micro\strain}.
